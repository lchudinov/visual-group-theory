# 2.2 Dihedral groups

Dihedral groups describe the symmetry of regular n-gons.

While cyclic groups describe 2D objects that only have rotational symmetry,
dihedral groups describe 2D objects that have rotational and reflective symmetry.

Dn - is a dihedral group that describes the symmetries of a regular n-gon.

All actions in Cn are also actions of Dn, but there are more than that.

Action of Dn:
- n rotations
- n reflections

Only two generators
- r - counterclockwise rotation by 2pi/n
- f - flip

Dn = {e, r, r2, ..., r(n-1), f, rf, r2f, ..., r(n-1)f}

(i.e. axes incident at `pi/n` radians), then `st` is a rotation by `2pi/n`.
