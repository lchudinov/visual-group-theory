## The symmetric group

The group of all permutations of n items is called the symmetric group and is denoted by Sn.

A transposition is a permutation that swaps two objects and fixes the rest.
In cycle notation, a transposition is just a 2-cycle, e.g. (i j)

The group Sn is generated by transpositions.
We only need adjacent transpositions to generate Sn.
`Sn = <(1 2), (2 3), ..., (n - 1 n)>`

(1 2 3) = (1 2)(2 3) = (1 2)(2 3)(2 3)(2 3) = (1 2)(2 3)(1 2)(1 2)

The parity of the number of transpositions of a fixed permutation is unique.
Even permutations and odd permutations.

Exactly half of the permutations in Sn are even, and the form a group called the
alternating group, denoted An.
